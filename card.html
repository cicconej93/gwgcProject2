Stegosaurus

Initially thought to lie flat on the Stegosaurus' back, it is now widely believed the bony proturions were arranged in two alternating rows and were used for display and thermoregulation.

  • Scientific Name: Stegosaurus stenops
  • Average Length: 9 m (29.5 ft)
  • Discovered By / Year: Marsh / 1887
  • Habitat: Fossils are typically found in the Morrison Formation, which is believed to have been a semiarid environment with wet and dry seasons.

The Stegosaurus lived 155 - 150 million years ago, during the Late Jurassic period. These herbivourous quadrupeds likely used their spiked tails to defend against predators.
